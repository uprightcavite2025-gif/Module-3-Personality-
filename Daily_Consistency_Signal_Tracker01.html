Daily Consistency Signal Tracker

Module 3 — Personality Trust

Purpose
Observe small credibility signals in everyday work
This tracker helps you observe small credibility signals during everyday work interactions.
Trust research shows that credibility is built through repeated behavior patterns, not single events (Mayer, Davis & Schoorman, 1995).
During the next 3–5 working days, take one minute at the end of your workday to reflect on how consistency appeared in your interactions.
You only need to record one short example per day.
The goal is not perfection.
The goal is awareness and repetition.
How to use
How to Use This Tracker
At the end of each workday:
  • Recall one situation where expectations, commitments, or decisions were involved.
  • Briefly describe what happened.
  • Identify the consistency signal that appeared.
  • Note what you observed in the interaction.
Short notes are enough. You are tracking patterns, not writing a report.
Signals to notice
Consistency Signals to Notice
  • Following through on commitments
  • Explaining changes clearly
  • Applying expectations consistently
  • Communicating transparently
  • Acknowledging mistakes
  • Acting in line with previous agreements
You may notice other signals as well. The point is to observe how credibility becomes visible.

Submission
Submission
Once your observations are complete, return to the course page and submit your worksheet.
Your responses remain confidential.

Why it matters
Why This Activity Matters
Credibility develops when people repeatedly observe alignment between words and actions.
Over time, these repeated signals allow others to form expectations about reliability.

When expectations become predictable, trust strengthens.
